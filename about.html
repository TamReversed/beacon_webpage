About ArgusPage

Always watching. Always clear.

In the age before gods learned to trust, Zeus had a problem. He needed someone to watch over what mattered most‚Äînot occasionally, not in shifts, not with dashboards that refreshed every thirty seconds. He needed vigilance as a permanent state of being. So he turned to the only creature in all of creation built for the job.

Argus Panoptes. The all-seeing. A giant with a hundred eyes scattered across his body, and when some of his eyes slept, the others stayed open. He literally could not stop watching. Vigilance was not his discipline. It was his architecture.

Hera, queen of the gods, trusted him with her single most valuable asset. Not a weapon. Not a kingdom. Information about the state of something she could not afford to lose. And Argus delivered. Every moment. Without fail. Without blinking.

That is not a myth. That is a product spec.

Your customers do not call your support line at 2 PM on a Tuesday when everything is fine. They show up at 3 AM on a Saturday when the payment API is returning 500s and their CEO is texting them in all caps. In that moment, they need a page that already knows. A page that was watching before anyone thought to ask. A page with a hundred eyes, some of which are always open.

That is ArgusPage.

We did not name it after a Greek god because it sounds cool on a landing page. We named it after the only being in three thousand years of mythology whose entire identity was the promise that nothing would go unseen. Not a warrior. Not a trickster. Not a king. A watcher. The original uptime monitor. The first status page.

Zeus eventually sent Hermes to kill Argus. You know what Hera did? She took his hundred eyes and placed them in the tail of the peacock‚Äîso they would be seen forever. Three millennia later, you can still see them.

Some things are built to keep watching long after everyone else has looked away.
